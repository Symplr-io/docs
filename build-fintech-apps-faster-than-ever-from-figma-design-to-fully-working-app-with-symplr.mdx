## How Does Symplr Work? A Step-by-Step Guide

Let’s walk through the process of building your fintech app with Symplr — from Figma design to a live, testable app.

### Step 1: Install the Symplr Figma Plugin

Start by installing the Symplr plugin from the Figma marketplace. This plugin lets you map UI components directly from your design, ensuring every button, screen, and input field transfers perfectly into Symplr.

### Step 2: Create a New Project in Symplr

Log in to your Symplr dashboard and click _Create New Project_. Name your app and prepare to import your Figma file.

### Step 3: Import Your Figma Design

Paste your Figma project URL and token into Symplr. If you don’t have a token yet, generate one by going to your Figma **Account Settings → Security → Generate New Token**. Once authorized, Symplr imports your full design, loading your app screens into a storyboard interface.

### Step 4: Import Your Workflows

Upload your Arazzo workflow files to define how your app behaves — from API calls to user interactions and business logic. This step adds intelligence to your app, turning static designs into interactive experiences.

### Step 5: Connect Workflows to Screens

Use Symplr’s visual interface to link workflows to UI components — for example, connecting a login workflow to your login screen button. Map the necessary data fields and events to ensure smooth interactions.

### Step 6: Build and Preview Your App

Click _Build New App_ and watch as Symplr generates a fully working app based on your design and workflows. Preview the app in your browser or on a mobile device to test every screen and function in real time.

### Step 7: Publish or Export Your Code

When your app is ready, connect your GitHub account and push the generated code with one click. You can continue development, deploy, or share with your team — Symplr keeps you flexible.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Do I need to know how to code to use Symplr?**\
No coding skills are required. Symplr’s no-code AI platform handles all the technical complexity. However, developers can export and customize the generated code if desired.

**Q: How secure is the connection between Symplr and Figma?**\
Symplr uses Figma’s official APIs and OAuth tokens to securely import designs. Tokens are managed by the user and can be revoked at any time.

**Q: Can I customize the app after it’s built in Symplr?**\
Yes! You can export your app’s source code to GitHub, allowing your development team to further customize and extend functionality as needed.

**Q: What types of workflows can I create?**\
Symplr supports Arazzo workflows — visual representations of API calls, logic, and event handling. You can automate everything from user authentication to complex payment processing.

**Q: How long does it take to build an app?**\
Most apps can be built and tested in a few hours, depending on the complexity of the design and workflows.

**Q: Can I integrate Symplr-built apps with existing backend systems?**\
Absolutely. Workflows can connect to your APIs, databases, and third-party services to build seamless integrations.

**Q: Is Symplr limited to fintech applications?**\
No, while Symplr excels in fintech, it is not restricted to this domain. Users have full control over the generated code, enabling them to extend and adapt applications for various industries beyond fintech.
